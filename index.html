Techno-Inequality

The difference in opportunity between those who have access to technology, and those who do not.

As with any inequality, technological inequality affects all aspects of life. Your ability to find a job, to receive an education, to change your current socioeconomic situation - they are all instrinsically affected by the technologies you have access to.

This is especially true for computer & internet access.

A study from 2007 found that "a 10 percentage-point increase in broadband penetration raises annual per-capita growth by 0.9-1.5 percentage points".

There are many variables for why the internet is a driving force for economic development, but arguably one key aspect is that it is a portal for easily accessible software that is especially useful for smaller businesses and entrepreneurs.
